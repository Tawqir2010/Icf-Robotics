What is FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC)?

FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC) is a robotics competition for students in grades 7-12 (ages 12-18), where teams practice engineering innovation as they design, build, and program robots to compete in exciting challenges.

üéØ How FTC Works

Season Structure

Each September, a new game challenge is revealed. Teams have the entire season to design, build, and program their robots to compete on a 12' x 12' playing field.

Competition Format

Matches consist of three parts: an autonomous period where robots operate on pre-programmed instructions, a driver-controlled period, and an end game. Teams compete in 2-vs-2 alliance matches.

Team Size

Teams have 2-15 members, all ages 12-18, guided by adult coaches and mentors who help students develop their skills.

Skills Developed

Programming (Java, Blocks), mechanical design, CAD, problem-solving, teamwork, community outreach, and engineering documentation.

What is FIRST LEGO League (FLL)?

FIRST LEGO League is a global STEM education program for children ages 4-16 that introduces science, technology, engineering, and math through hands-on, LEGO-based robotics learning and problem-solving challenges.

üéØ How FLL Works

Season Timeline

Each August, a new challenge theme is released. Teams work for 8+ weeks building, programming, and researching before competing at regional events in November.

Three Divisions

Discover (ages 4-6): Playful intro with LEGO DUPLO
Explore (ages 6-10): Engineering basics with SPIKE Essential
Challenge (ages 9-16): Competitive robotics with SPIKE Prime

Four Components

Robot Game missions, Innovation Project research, Core Values judging, and Robot Design presentation.

Skills Developed

LEGO building, block programming, research skills, presentation skills, teamwork, and gracious professionalism.

üèÜ FLL Challenge Division

üéÆ Robot Game

Design, build, and program a LEGO robot to complete mission tasks on a themed game mat in 2.5-minute matches.

üí° Innovation Project

Research a real-world problem related to the season theme and develop a creative solution to present to judges.

ü§ù Core Values

Teams are judged on teamwork, gracious professionalism, and "Coopertition" (cooperative competition).

üîß Robot Design

Technical judging of mechanical design, programming strategy, and innovation in robot construction.

üí° What Students Learn

  • Build and program robots using LEGO technology
  • Learn coding with block-based programming
  • Conduct research on real-world problems
  • Present solutions to judges and audiences
  • Work collaboratively in teams with diverse skills
  • Practice critical thinking and problem-solving
  • Develop confidence and communication skills
